ESP32 Smart Plant Monitor

Build a smart plant monitor that reads soil moisture and tells you when your plant is thirsty — right on your computer screen.

BeginnerAges 10+45–60 minUnder $15Parent Safe

🌱 Project Mission

Build Your Smart Plant Monitor

📖The Story

Your plant cannot text you when it is thirsty — but you can build something that checks for it.

In this project you stick a soil moisture sensor in the dirt, connect it to your ESP32, and watch live moisture readings appear on your screen. When the soil gets dry, your code speaks up before the leaves start to droop.

Greenhouses and smart gardens use the same trick — sense first, water only when needed.

🧒Explain Like I'm 12

The soil sensor is like a sponge detective. It checks how wet the dirt is and sends a number to the ESP32. Your board reads that number and tells your laptop whether the plant is happy or needs a drink — like a plant nurse on duty every two seconds.

For Parents & Teachers

This project uses 3.3 V USB power only — no mains electricity and no water pump in the starter build. Children should have an adult nearby for USB connections and breadboard wiring. Unplug before changing wires. Do not submerge the electronics — only the sensor probe belongs in soil.

🛠What You Will Build

A breadboard circuit with a soil moisture sensor and ESP32. Your code reads analog moisture values every two seconds, prints them to Serial Monitor, and warns you when the soil looks dry.

🔗Wiring

Use a 3-pin soil moisture module. Keep the ESP32 on the breadboard and only the probe in the soil. Match each wire before plugging in USB.

  1. 1

    Unplug USB. Place the ESP32 and soil moisture module on your breadboard.

  2. 2

    Connect VCC on the module to 3.3 V on the ESP32.

  3. 3

    Connect GND on the module to GND on the ESP32.

  4. 4

    Connect the sensor signal pin to GPIO34 on the ESP32.

  5. 5

    Insert only the probe end into a cup of damp soil or a potted plant — not the electronics.

  6. 6

    Double-check all three wires, then plug in USB.

⌨️Code

Copy into Arduino IDE. Install any libraries noted in the component guides first.

smart_plant_monitor.ino 
#define MOISTURE_PIN 34
const int DRY_THRESHOLD = 3000;

void setup() {
  Serial.begin(115200);
  Serial.println("Smart Plant Monitor ready!");
}

void loop() {
  int moisture = analogRead(MOISTURE_PIN);

  Serial.print("Soil moisture raw: ");
  Serial.println(moisture);

  if (moisture > DRY_THRESHOLD) {
    Serial.println("Plant is thirsty — time to water!");
  } else {
    Serial.println("Soil looks okay.");
  }

  delay(2000);
}

✨Expected Output

Open Serial Monitor at 115200 baud. Every 2 seconds you should see:

Soil moisture raw: 1850 Soil looks okay.

When the soil dries out, the raw number rises and you should see:

Soil moisture raw: 3200 Plant is thirsty — time to water!

Try watering the plant lightly — the raw value should drop within a minute.

🔧Troubleshooting

  • Raw value stuck at 0 or 4095 Check VCC, GND, and signal wiring. Confirm GPIO34 in code matches your wire.
  • No output in Serial Monitor Set baud rate to 115200 and select the correct COM port for your ESP32.
  • Always says thirsty or always says okay Change DRY_THRESHOLD in code. Test with dry soil and wet soil to find a value that works for your sensor.

⚠️Common Mistakes

  • Putting the whole module in water or soil instead of just the probe.
  • Signal wire on the wrong GPIO pin compared to your code.
  • Using a charge-only USB cable that cannot upload code.
  • Expecting instant changes — wait a minute after watering for the reading to settle.

⬆️Upgrade Ideas

  • Add an OLED display to show moisture without a laptop
  • Connect a relay on GPIO26 to switch a small water pump when soil is dry (Advanced — adult supervision)
  • Log readings to a chart over Wi-Fi (Advanced)
  • Combine with Mission 2 DHT22 to track temperature and soil together
